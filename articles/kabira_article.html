Kabira's Article

October 23th, 2016 by Kabira Suleman.

The Applied Software Engineering course at Cardiff University has been a constant learning journey for me - having had no former programming experience I find it very useful to makes notes of even the most trivial things such as the syntax of a language. I find I often need the logical foundation or explanation of why a method work in order to grasp an idea and make it ‘stick’. The course so far has also been very informative - it's very hands on and we are producing code every lesson rather than solely listening to somebody talking without any practice or input. The interactivity of the course makes it good particularly for ma as i am a kinesthetic learner. The course has been full of positives for me and I enjoy the challenge of programming and appreciate the rewarding feeling when you persevere and your code runs - I see I am quite behind a majority of a class but I am confident that will a lot of hard work I will follow the curriculum and advance my skills in programming.The website is going well, I have learnt a lot of things from Faisal and he has spent a lot of time explaining things thoroughly to me which I really appreciate. Some of the things I learnt from him include website positioning with use of padding and margins and how to create a container which instantly elevates the presentation of the website and makes a critical change.
